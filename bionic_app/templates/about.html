About Bionic Hand

What is a Bionic Hand?

A bionic hand is an advanced prosthetic device designed to replicate the functionality of a human hand. It combines mechanical engineering, electronics, and software to provide users with natural hand movements and control.

Modern bionic hands use sensors to detect muscle signals (EMG), process them through microcontrollers, and actuate servo motors to move individual fingers with precision.

How Does It Work?

  1. Signal Detection: EMG sensors detect electrical signals from residual muscles in the user's arm.
  2. Signal Processing: A microcontroller (Arduino/ESP32) processes these signals and determines the intended movement.
  3. Actuation: Servo motors receive commands and move the fingers to perform the desired action (grip, pinch, open, close).
  4. Feedback: Force sensors provide feedback to prevent excessive grip force and protect objects.

Key Features

  • 5 Individually Controlled Fingers
  • EMG Sensor Integration
  • Multiple Grip Patterns
  • Force Feedback System
  • Lightweight Design
  • Rechargeable Battery
  • Wireless Control Option
  • Customizable Settings

Real-Life Applications

Bionic hands have transformed the lives of amputees by providing:

  • Daily Activities: Eating, writing, typing, and personal care
  • Professional Work: Operating tools, typing, and precision tasks
  • Sports & Recreation: Adapted sports and recreational activities
  • Social Interaction: Handshakes, gestures, and natural communication
